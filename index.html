New Yorkers May Have to Find Hospice Alternatives

The hospice industry has seen a nationwide downturn in recent years as government funding for industry services has come under scrutiny in Washington. Budget sequestration limited funding for hospice care services in 2013, and in 2014 the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services began implementing a four-year 3.5 percent annual reduction to the Medicare base payment for home healthcare services. The move was meant to help offset the costs of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, but companies began exiting the hospice industry in anticipation of these cuts.

Historically, New Yorkers have been slower to embrace hospice care. The uncertainty of future funding for hospice providers has limited national industry revenue growth to just 1.1 percent per year on average. According to the National Association for Home Care and Hospice, this reduction is expected to cause three-fourths of all hospice and home healthcare companies in the United States to be unable to operate profitably by 2017. IBISWorld expects industry revenue to fall from $17.7 billion in 2015 to $15.8 billion in 2020.
